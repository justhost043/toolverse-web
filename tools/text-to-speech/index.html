Voice personas explained

Narrator uses a slower rate and lower pitch for a dramatic, authoritative sound — ideal for audiobook content, documentary-style scripts, or formal presentations. Assistant is the most natural-sounding everyday voice, balanced for instructions, tutorials, and conversational content. News Anchor speaks faster and crisply — good for time-sensitive announcements or summaries. Teacher uses a higher pitch and slower pace for clarity — ideal for educational content or step-by-step explanations. Storyteller is the most expressive, best for creative writing and children's content. Whisperer is soft and slow — perfect for meditation, relaxation scripts, or ASMR-style content.

Download audio

Click the Download button after pressing play — your browser will record the speech output and offer it as a WebM audio file. The download feature uses the browser's MediaRecorder API, which works in Chrome and Edge. Safari and Firefox may not support audio download.

Tips for best results

Shorter sentences with commas and periods produce the most natural rhythm — the TTS engine pauses at punctuation marks. For long texts, use the 1.25x speed to save time while keeping clarity. The Pause Depth control adds extra pauses between paragraphs, making long content easier to follow. Try different installed system voices (via the Voice selector) — each sounds distinctly different.
