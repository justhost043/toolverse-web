Uses for text to speech

Text to speech is useful for proofreading — hearing your own writing read aloud exposes awkward phrasing and errors that your eyes skip over. It helps people with dyslexia or visual impairments consume written content more easily. Language learners use it to hear correct pronunciation. Busy people use it to "read" articles while commuting or exercising. Content creators use it to test how scripts sound before recording.

About the voices

This tool uses your browser's built-in Web Speech API, which accesses the text-to-speech voices installed on your operating system. The number and quality of available voices depends on your device and browser. Windows, macOS, iOS and Android each come with different built-in voices. Chrome and Edge on desktop typically have the largest selection of voices available.
