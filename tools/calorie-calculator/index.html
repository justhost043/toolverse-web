What is TDEE?

TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is the total number of calories your body burns in a day, including both your resting metabolism and all physical activity. It's calculated by multiplying your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) by an activity factor. Knowing your TDEE gives you a starting point for calorie targets: eat below it to lose weight, at it to maintain, and above it to gain weight.

What is BMR?

BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the number of calories your body needs to maintain basic life functions at complete rest — breathing, circulation, cell production, and temperature regulation. This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation (1990), which is considered the most accurate for estimating BMR in most adults.

About the macro split

The recommended macro split shown here follows a standard balanced diet guideline: approximately 30% protein, 45% carbohydrates, and 25% fat. This is a starting point — individual needs vary based on goals, health conditions, and personal preference. Athletes and those doing strength training typically benefit from a higher protein intake (35–40% of calories).
