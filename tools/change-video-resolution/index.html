Video resolution and aspect ratio

Resolution determines the number of pixels in your video. Higher resolution means sharper image but larger file size. 1080p (1920×1080) is the standard for YouTube and most streaming platforms. 720p is a good balance for web sharing. When changing resolution, maintaining aspect ratio prevents stretching — black bars are added to fill the remaining space if the target resolution has a different ratio.
