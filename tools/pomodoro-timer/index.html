What is the Pomodoro Technique?

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. The method uses a timer to break work into 25-minute focus intervals (called "pomodoros") separated by 5-minute short breaks. After completing four pomodoros, you take a longer 15–30 minute break. The technique helps maintain focus by creating a sense of urgency, reduces the impact of interruptions, and builds awareness of how you actually spend your time.

Why 25 minutes?

Research on attention spans suggests that most people can sustain focused concentration for about 20–30 minutes before needing a mental rest. The 25-minute interval is short enough to create urgency (making procrastination harder) but long enough to make meaningful progress on most tasks. The frequent breaks prevent mental fatigue from accumulating over long work sessions.
