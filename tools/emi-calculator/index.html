What is EMI?

EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment) is the fixed amount you pay to a lender every month until your loan is fully paid off. Each EMI payment covers both a portion of the principal (the original loan amount) and the interest for that period. In the early months of a loan, a larger portion of each EMI goes toward interest; as the balance decreases, more of each payment goes toward principal.

How is EMI calculated?

The standard EMI formula is: EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n − 1), where P is the principal amount, r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate divided by 12), and n is the number of monthly payments. For example, a $100,000 loan at 8.5% annual interest for 20 years gives a monthly EMI of approximately $868.

How to reduce your total interest paid

The most effective way to reduce total interest is to make extra payments toward the principal whenever possible. Even one extra payment per year can reduce a 20-year loan term by several years. A shorter loan tenure also reduces total interest significantly — a 15-year loan typically costs much less in total interest than a 20-year loan, though the monthly EMI will be higher.
