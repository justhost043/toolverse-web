Why use a browser-based QR code generator?

Most "free" QR generators online quietly add a logo watermark, require an account once you exceed a handful of scans, or route your data through their servers before showing you a result. This tool works differently: the QR code is built entirely with JavaScript running on your own device. Nothing you type — your URL, your Wi-Fi password, your text — is ever transmitted anywhere. The moment you close the tab, no record of it exists on any server, because it was never sent to one in the first place.

Common uses for QR codes

QR codes have become a everyday bridge between print and digital. A restaurant prints one on a table tent to link to its menu. A small business owner puts one on a flyer to link straight to their Instagram. A freelancer adds one to a printed invoice linking to their portfolio. Event organizers use the Wi-Fi type to let guests join a network without typing a password. Because the code itself contains the full data, it keeps working indefinitely, regardless of which tool generated it.
