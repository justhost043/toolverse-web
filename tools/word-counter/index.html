Why does word count matter?

Word count requirements exist across many contexts: academic essays and assignments have minimum or maximum requirements; SEO content typically needs 1,500+ words for competitive topics; LinkedIn posts perform best under 300 words; Twitter limits posts to 280 characters; email subject lines should stay under 50 characters for mobile visibility. This tool helps you hit your target precisely.

What is Flesch readability?

The Flesch Reading Ease score measures how easy text is to read, based on average sentence length and average word length (syllables per word). A score of 90–100 means very easy (5th grade level — think children's books). A score of 0–30 means very difficult (university graduate level — academic papers). For most web content, aim for 60–70 (8th–9th grade level) to reach the widest audience.

How is reading time calculated?

Reading time uses 238 words per minute — the average silent reading speed for adults reading non-fiction content. Speaking time uses 150 words per minute — the typical pace for presentations and voice-overs. Both are approximations; technical content with complex terminology will take longer.
