Why use a word counter?

Whether you're writing a blog post, a college essay, a product description, or a tweet, knowing your word and character count matters. SEO articles typically target specific lengths, academic submissions enforce word limits, and social platforms cap character counts. This tool gives you all those numbers at once — plus a reading time estimate so you can match your content length to your audience's attention span.

How reading time is calculated

The reading time shown assumes an average adult reading speed of 200 words per minute, which is the widely-used standard for general web content. For technical or complex writing, the real reading time is typically higher; for simple, scannable content, it may be lower.

What is keyword density?

Keyword density shows how often a particular word appears as a percentage of total words. SEO writers track this to avoid "keyword stuffing" — overusing the same word to the point where search engines flag the content as low quality. Most guidelines recommend keeping any single keyword below 2–3% of total content.
