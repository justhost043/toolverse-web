How audio joining works

Audio joining concatenates multiple audio tracks end-to-end into a single file. The crossfade option overlaps the end of one track with the start of the next, blending them smoothly — this prevents jarring cuts between tracks and is the standard technique in music playlists, podcast episodes, and audio books.
