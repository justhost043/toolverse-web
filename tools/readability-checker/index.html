    What is Flesch Reading Ease?

    The Flesch Reading Ease score is a well-established formula developed by Rudolf Flesch in 1948. It gives a score from 0 to 100 based on average sentence length and average syllables per word. A score of 60–70 is considered ideal for general web content — roughly equivalent to an 8th-9th grade reading level. News articles typically score 60–70. Academic papers often score 30–50. Simple children's books score above 90.

    How to improve readability

    The most effective changes are: shorten long sentences (aim for an average of 15-20 words), replace complex multi-syllable words with simpler alternatives, use active voice instead of passive constructions, and break large paragraphs into smaller ones. Even very technical content can be made clearer without dumbing it down — the goal is reducing the cognitive load on the reader, not simplifying the ideas themselves.
