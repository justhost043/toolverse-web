Simple vs Compound Interest

Simple interest is calculated only on the original principal. If you invest $10,000 at 8% simple interest for 10 years, you earn $800 per year — $8,000 total. Compound interest calculates interest on both the principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods. The same $10,000 at 8% compounded monthly grows to $22,196 — $12,196 in interest rather than $8,000. That difference is why Einstein reportedly called compound interest "the eighth wonder of the world."

Why compounding frequency matters

The more frequently interest compounds, the more you earn. Daily compounding yields slightly more than monthly, which yields more than annual. For most savings accounts and investments, monthly compounding is the standard. The difference between monthly and daily compounding is small, but over decades it can add meaningful amounts to a large portfolio.
