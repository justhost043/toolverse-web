Extracting audio streams from video

Every video container (MP4, MKV, AVI) holds separate streams — video frames, audio data, and sometimes subtitles. Extracting audio means FFmpeg reads the audio stream and writes it to a new file, leaving video data behind. This does not re-compress the audio if you choose a lossless format like WAV, preserving the exact original audio quality.
