What is WPM?

WPM (Words Per Minute) is the standard measure of typing speed. By convention, one "word" is defined as 5 keystrokes, including spaces — this standardizes the measurement regardless of whether you're typing long or short words. The average adult typist reaches 40–50 WPM. Professional typists typically achieve 65–75 WPM. Over 80 WPM is considered fast, and competitive typists often exceed 120 WPM.

How to improve your typing speed

The most effective technique is to focus on accuracy first, then speed. Typing fast with many errors actually slows you down because you have to correct mistakes. Learn proper finger placement on the home row (ASDF JKL;) and resist the urge to look at your keyboard — touch typing without looking at the keys is what separates fast typists from slow ones. Consistent daily practice of even 10–15 minutes produces measurable improvement within weeks.
