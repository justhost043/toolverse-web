What is aspect ratio?

Aspect ratio is the proportional relationship between the width and height of an image or screen, expressed as two numbers separated by a colon. A 1920×1080 image has a 16:9 ratio — for every 16 units wide, it is 9 units tall. The ratio stays the same regardless of the actual pixel dimensions, which is why 1280×720 and 3840×2160 are both "16:9".

Why aspect ratio matters

Resizing an image without maintaining its aspect ratio causes distortion — people and objects look stretched or squashed. Knowing the correct ratio lets you scale to any size while keeping the image looking natural.
