What is audio bitrate and sample rate?

Bitrate measures how much data is used per second of audio — higher bitrate means better quality and larger file size. 320 kbps is CD quality for MP3. Sample rate is how many times per second the audio is sampled — 44,100 Hz (44.1 kHz) is the CD standard, and 48,000 Hz is the standard for video production. Changing the sample rate affects both file size and compatibility with different devices and software.
