When do you need to write numbers as words?

Legal documents (cheques, contracts, deeds) require amounts written in both numerals and words to prevent fraud — it's much harder to alter "fifty thousand" than to change "50,000" to "500,000". Academic writing often requires numbers under ten to be spelled out. Formal writing, invitations and announcements typically spell out numbers for readability and style.

How large a number can this convert?

This converter handles numbers up to the hundreds of trillions — more than enough for any practical financial or legal document. For context, the total US national debt is in the tens of trillions, so this covers any real-world use case.
