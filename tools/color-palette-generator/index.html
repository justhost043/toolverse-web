Color harmony schemes explained

Complementary colors sit opposite each other on the color wheel — they create high contrast and vibrant designs. Triadic uses three colors evenly spaced at 120° intervals, creating balanced yet colorful palettes. Analogous uses colors adjacent on the wheel — they're naturally harmonious and found frequently in nature. Split-complementary takes a color and uses the two colors adjacent to its complement — it has the contrast of complementary but is less tension-creating. Tetradic uses four colors forming a rectangle on the wheel — the richest scheme but hardest to balance. Monochromatic uses different shades and tints of a single hue — always cohesive and elegant.
