What is word frequency analysis used for?

Word frequency analysis reveals the vocabulary patterns in any piece of text. Writers use it to identify overused words — if "very" or "just" appears 40 times in an essay, that's a signal to vary your language. SEO professionals use it to check keyword density in content. Researchers use it to compare writing styles across authors or time periods. Teachers use it to analyze student essays for vocabulary breadth.

Stop words

Stop words are extremely common words like "the", "a", "is", "in", "and" that appear in almost every text. They carry little semantic meaning and, when included, dominate the frequency list. Excluding them reveals the meaningful content words — the nouns, verbs, and adjectives that actually define what the text is about.
