What is character frequency analysis?

Character frequency analysis counts how often each letter, word, or symbol appears in a piece of text. It's one of the oldest techniques in cryptanalysis — the Caesar cipher was cracked by noticing that the most common letter in ciphertext likely corresponds to E, the most frequent letter in English. Modern uses include analyzing writing style, comparing language patterns, and identifying the language of an unknown text based on its letter distribution.

English letter frequency

In typical English text, the most common letters in order are: E, T, A, O, I, N, S, H, R, D, L, C, U, M, W, F, G, Y, P, B, V, K, J, X, Q, Z. If your analyzed text shows a significantly different distribution, it may indicate that the text is not in standard English, or that it has been deliberately constrained (like a lipogram that avoids a specific letter).
