What is audio dynamic compression?

Dynamic compression reduces the volume difference between the loudest and quietest parts of audio. The threshold sets the level above which compression kicks in. The ratio controls how much compression is applied (4:1 means audio 4dB above the threshold becomes 1dB louder). Make-up gain then raises the overall level back up. The result is more consistent, "louder" sounding audio — the standard for podcasts, YouTube videos, and music streaming.
