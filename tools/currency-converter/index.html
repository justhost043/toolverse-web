How exchange rates work

Exchange rates represent the value of one currency relative to another. They fluctuate constantly based on supply and demand, which is driven by international trade, investment flows, economic data releases, and central bank policies. The rates shown here are mid-market rates — the midpoint between the buying and selling rates used by banks. Banks and exchange services typically add a margin of 1–3% above the mid-market rate, which is their profit.

Why do exchange rates change daily?

Currency markets are open 24 hours a day, 5 days a week and trade over $7 trillion daily — making them the largest financial market in the world. Rates move based on inflation reports, interest rate decisions, political events, and market sentiment. Small changes in rate expectations can cause significant currency movements overnight.
