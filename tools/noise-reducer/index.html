Noise gate vs noise reduction

A noise gate silences audio that falls below a set threshold, treating anything below that level as noise. This is effective for recordings with clear pauses between speech — the gate closes during silence and opens when you speak. True noise reduction (spectral subtraction) is more complex and requires a "noise profile" sample. For best results with this tool, set the threshold just above your background noise level and use smoothing to avoid harsh gating artifacts.
