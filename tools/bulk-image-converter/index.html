Which format should I convert to?

JPEG — best for photographs and images with many colors. Produces small files with good visual quality. Does not support transparency. PNG — lossless format best for screenshots, logos, and images with sharp edges or transparency. Larger files than JPEG. WebP — modern format supported by all current browsers. Produces 25–35% smaller files than JPEG at equal quality, and supports transparency like PNG. Ideal for websites and apps.

How many images can I convert at once?

There is no hard limit — the tool processes images sequentially to avoid running out of memory. However, very large batches (100+ high-resolution images) may take a few minutes. Each image is drawn onto an HTML5 Canvas and re-encoded in your browser. The process uses your CPU and RAM, not a server.
